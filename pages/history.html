The Demel Museum

Immerse yourself in the world of Demel, the art of confectionery and Family history.

In addition to the wide range of products offered by Demel, the artistic displays, which were originally established by Berzeviczy-Palavicini, are particularly impressive. To this day, it is still a tradition to decorate the displays with new decors according to the season and events.

If you would like to see more display decorations and get a taste of history, you can visit the vaults of Demel, which not only contain many a "best of" display art, but also valuable historical objects dating back to the beginnings of Demel.

Opening hours: every Friday, 10.00 to 12.00 hrs

You will be looked after by two local guides. Admission per person: € 4,-

Our Story

The Demel Vienna

Kohlmarkt 14, 1010 Wien

Founded in 1786, the traditional confectionery Demel looks back on a history of centuries. Even today, the Demel salons still present themselves in the beautiful rococo style of the architects Portois & Fix, one of the most distinguished and solid furnishing companies in the monarchy.

Even Emperor Franz Josef I, who was already raving about confectionery as a child, and his Elisabeth, who could not get enough of the famous violet sorbet, had Demel's delicacies delivered to the Hofburg. The Hofzuckerbäckerei quickly became a palace with a sweet tooth and Demel has always been a popular meeting place for the aristocracy and the Viennese bourgeoisie. It did not lose this status even after the downfall of the monarchy, because time stood still at Demel. Anna Demel, who was the first woman in Austria to be awarded the title of "Kommerzialrat" in 1957, kept a strict eye on the old order and so you can still feel in Demel today what was then.

After Anna's death, it was above all her son-in-law, the artist and pupil of the Wiener Werkstätte, Baron Federico von Berzeviczy-Pallavicini, who shaped the appearance of Demel with his fascinating displays and playful designs. These designs still adorn numerous packaging today, as do the Wiener Werkstätte motifs which can still be found in Demel.
